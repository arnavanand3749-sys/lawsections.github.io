What does Section 379 IPC say?

Section 379 of the Indian Penal Code defines and punishes theft. In simple terms, theft occurs when a person dishonestly takes someone else's movable property out of their possession without consent and with the intention of permanently depriving them of it.

Plain-English definition

An act will fall under Section 379 when:

  • There is taking of movable property;
  • The property is taken dishonestly (mens rea);
  • The property is taken without the consent of the owner or person in possession;
  • There is an intention to permanently deprive the owner of the property.

Punishment under Section 379

Section 379 prescribes:

  • Imprisonment which may extend to three years, or
  • Fine, or
  • Both.

The severity of punishment depends on the facts, value of property, and whether other aggravated sections apply (for example, theft after preparation for causing death or grievous hurt, dacoity, etc.).

Difference: Theft vs Robbery vs Extortion

Theft (Section 379) is dishonest taking without consent. Robbery involves theft plus force or threat (Sections 390–392). Extortion (Section 383) involves obtaining property by threat or coercion — the means and immediacy of force distinguish these offences.

Simple examples

Example A (Theft): A picks up a parked bicycle belonging to B and leaves with it intending never to return it. → Section 379 applies.

Example B (Robbery): A snatches a phone from B by threatening violence. → Robbery charges apply (not just Section 379).

Example C (Household theft): C enters D's house without permission and steals jewellery. → Sections for house-trespass and theft may apply (Sections 380, 457, etc.).

Landmark cases & legal notes

  • R. v. Hale — classic common-law discussion on appropriation (useful for comparative context).
  • K. M. Nanavati v. State of Mumbai — while primarily a homicide case, courts discuss mens rea principles relevant across offences.
  • Court focus typically examines possession, consent, and intention to permanently deprive when deciding theft cases.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is Section 379 IPC?
Section 379 defines theft and prescribes punishment for dishonestly taking movable property without consent with intent to permanently deprive.
What is the punishment for theft?
Imprisonment up to three years, or fine, or both.
How is theft different from robbery?
Robbery includes force or threat of force during the act of theft — making the offence more serious and attracting heavier punishment.
Can a theft case be compoundable?
Minor thefts may be compoundable in some circumstances; serious thefts and those involving aggravating factors typically are not compoundable.
