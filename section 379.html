Offences Against Property

Section 379 IPC

Punishment for Theft

What does Section 379 say?

Section 379 prescribes the punishment for the offence of **Theft**, which is defined in Section 378 of the IPC.

Theft is the dishonest moving of **movable property** out of the possession of any person **without that person's consent**, with the **intention to take it away permanently**.

Essential Ingredients of Theft (Section 378)

To establish a case of theft, the following elements must be present:

  • **Dishonest Intention (Mens Rea):** The offender must intend to cause wrongful gain or wrongful loss.
  • **Movable Property:** The subject of the theft must be a movable thing.
  • **Taken Out of Possession:** The property must be moved, even slightly, from the victim's possession.
  • **Without Consent:** The movement must be done without the owner's permission.
  • **Moving:** There must be a physical movement or taking of the property.

Punishment Prescribed (Section 379)

  • **Imprisonment** which may extend to **three years**, or
  • **Fine**, or
  • **Both**.

The punishment severity depends on the value of the property and any associated aggravating factors (e.g., house-trespass).

Key Facts at a Glance

Feature Details
Nature of Offence Cognizable (police can arrest without a warrant)
Bailability Bailable (depending on the facts and relevant State amendments)
Trial Court Any Magistrate's Court (usually Judicial Magistrate First Class)
Aggravated Sections Sections 380 (Theft in dwelling-house), 381 (Theft by servant), 382 (Theft after preparation for hurt).

Theft vs. Robbery vs. Extortion

This difference is crucial in law:

  • **Theft (Sec 379):** Taking property **without force or threat**.
  • **Extortion (Sec 383):** Obtaining property by **putting a person in fear** of injury to compel delivery.
  • **Robbery (Sec 390):** Theft becomes robbery if the offender uses or attempts to use **force/violence** to commit the theft or carry away the property.

Simple Examples

Example A (Theft):

A reaches into B's pocket in a crowded market and picks his wallet without B knowing. This is pure Theft (Section 379).

Example B (Aggravated Theft/Robbery):

A enters B's locked car (house-trespass) and takes the music system. This would be **Theft in a Dwelling House** (Section 380), which carries a higher sentence.

Legal Notes & Definitions

  • **Movability:** Land/immovable property cannot be stolen. However, things *attached* to the earth (like trees) become movable property when severed (cut down), and theft may then apply.
  • **Temporarily Taking:** If the intention was to take the property only temporarily (e.g., borrowing a car without permission and intending to return it), it may not constitute theft, as the intention to *permanently deprive* is missing.

For the official definition of theft, refer to Section 378 IPC.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the punishment for theft?

Up to 3 years imprisonment, or a fine, or both.

Is theft (379 IPC) bailable?

Yes, generally it is a bailable offence, meaning the accused has a right to be released on bail by the police or the court.

Can a theft case be compounded?

Yes, simple theft (Section 379) is generally compoundable by the owner of the property with the permission of the court (under CrPC Section 320), allowing for a compromise.
