What does Section 304B IPC say?

Section 304B addresses the offence of dowry death. It applies where a woman dies under certain circumstances soon after marriage and it is shown that she was subjected to cruelty or harassment by her husband or his relatives in connection with any demand for dowry.

Plain-English definition

An offence falls under Section 304B when all these elements are present:

  • Death of a woman;
  • Death occurred within seven years of marriage (the statutory time period for dowry-death presumption);
  • The death occurred under suspicious circumstances (for example, burns, bodily injury, or unnatural causes); and
  • There is evidence that soon before her death she was subjected to cruelty or harassment by her husband or his relatives in connection with a demand for dowry.

Punishment under Section 304B

When Section 304B is made out, the punishment prescribed is:

  • Rigorous imprisonment for a term of not less than seven years, and
  • That term may extend to imprisonment for life.

Dowry death is a very serious, non-bailable, cognizable and non-compoundable offence. Courts treat such cases with strict scrutiny.

How is Section 304B different from Section 302 (murder)?

Section 302 punishes murder generally. Section 304B is a specific offence for dowry death with a statutory presumption and a mandatory minimum sentence (7 years). If facts show murder beyond doubt, both sections can be invoked depending on evidence.

Simple examples

Example A (Dowry death): A woman dies from burns within two years of marriage. Before her death she was repeatedly harassed over dowry by her husband and in-laws. → Section 304B may be attracted.

Example B (Not dowry death): A woman dies of a sudden natural illness with no evidence of harassment or dowry demand. → Section 304B will likely not apply; other sections may be considered if foul play is suspected.

Legal notes

  • Court examines timing (within seven years), nature of death, and evidence of harassment/dowry demand 'soon before' death.
  • A presumption is available to the prosecution but it is rebuttable — the defence can present evidence to show otherwise.
  • Dowry death charges are often tried with related offences (cruelty under Section 498A IPC, murder under Section 302, etc.).

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is Section 304B IPC?
Section 304B is the IPC offence for dowry death — when a woman dies under suspicious circumstances within seven years of marriage and had been subjected to cruelty or harassment related to dowry.
What is the punishment for dowry death?
Rigorous imprisonment for not less than seven years, which may extend to life imprisonment.
Is dowry death bailable?
No. Dowry death is generally non-bailable and cognizable.
Can Section 304B and 302 be applied together?
Yes. Depending on the facts, the prosecution may charge under 304B and/or 302. 304B carries a statutory presumption which assists the prosecution when the required elements are proved.
