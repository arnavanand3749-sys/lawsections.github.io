Offences Against Women

Section 304B IPC

Dowry Death

What does Section 304B say?

"Where the death of a woman is caused by burns, bodily injury, or occurs otherwise than under normal circumstances within seven years of marriage, and it is shown that soon before her death she was subjected to cruelty or harassment for dowry, such death shall be called ‘dowry death’."

An offence falls under Section 304B when all four of these elements are present:

  • Death of a woman occurs.
  • The death happened within seven years of marriage.
  • The death was unnatural (burns, injury, suspicious circumstances).
  • There is evidence she was subjected to cruelty/harassment by her husband or in-laws soon before death specifically regarding dowry demands.

Punishment Prescribed

  • Minimum: Rigorous imprisonment for not less than 7 years.
  • Maximum: May extend to imprisonment for life.

Note: This is a non-bailable, cognizable, and non-compoundable offence. Courts treat these cases with strict scrutiny.

Key Facts at a Glance

Feature Details
Nature of Offence Cognizable, Non-bailable, Non-compoundable
Legal Presumption If facts are proved, court shall presume the husband/relatives caused the death (Sec 113B Evidence Act).
Trial Court Session Court / Special Courts for women

Difference: Section 304B vs Section 302

Section 302 is for Murder generally. Section 304B is a specific law created to tackle dowry deaths. It has a unique "statutory presumption" (the burden of proof shifts to the accused if harassment is proved). If clear intent to kill is found, an accused can be charged under both sections.

Simple Examples

Example A (Dowry Death):

A woman dies from burns in her kitchen 2 years after marriage. Neighbors testify that her husband beat her daily demanding a motorcycle. Since it is within 7 years and involves dowry harassment soon before death, Section 304B applies.

Example B (Not Dowry Death):

A woman dies of a heart attack 10 years after marriage. There were no demands for money. This does not fall under Section 304B because the 7-year limit has passed and the death was natural.

Important Legal Notes

  • "Soon Before Death": There is no fixed time limit for this. Courts decide based on facts. However, the harassment must not be stale; there must be a live link between the cruelty and the death.
  • Burden of Proof: Once the prosecution proves the woman died unnaturally within 7 years and was harassed for dowry, the burden shifts to the husband to prove he is innocent.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the punishment for dowry death?

Minimum 7 years rigorous imprisonment, extending up to life imprisonment.

Is dowry death bailable?

No. It is a serious non-bailable offence. Bail is rarely granted by lower courts.

Can Section 304B and 302 be applied together?

Yes. If the police find evidence that the husband actively killed the wife (e.g., set her on fire), they will charge him with both Murder (302) and Dowry Death (304B).
