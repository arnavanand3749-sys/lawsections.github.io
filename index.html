Plain English explanations of the Indian Penal Code & CrPC.

Section 302 — Punishment for murder IPC

Plain English: Intentional killing is murder. The punishment is severe: either life imprisonment or the death penalty.

Statutory Text Source

Whoever commits murder shall be punished with death or imprisonment for life.

Section 307 — Attempt to murder IPC

Plain English: An act done with the intention or knowledge to cause death—but which fails to result in death. It attracts up to 10 years in jail. If hurt is caused, the penalty may increase to life imprisonment.

Statutory Text Source

Whoever does any act with such intention or knowledge, and under such circumstances that, if he by that act caused death, he would be guilty of murder, shall be punished with imprisonment for life, or with imprisonment of either description which may extend to ten years...

Section 376 — Punishment for rape High Severity

Plain English: Sexual intercourse without consent. The law prescribes severe rigorous imprisonment (minimum 10 years to life) and fines. Enhanced punishments exist for crimes against minors or by authority figures.

Whoever... commits rape, shall be punished with rigorous imprisonment of either description for a term which shall not be less than ten years, but which may extend to imprisonment for life...

Section 420 — Cheating & Fraud IPC

Plain English: Serious forms of cheating where someone is deceived into handing over property or valuables. Punishable by jail up to 7 years.

Whoever cheats and thereby dishonestly induces the person deceived to deliver any property to any person... shall be punished with imprisonment... for a term which may extend to seven years...

Section 379 — Theft IPC

Plain English: Taking someone else's movable property dishonestly. Penalty can be up to 3 years jail, fine, or both.

Whoever commits theft shall be punished with imprisonment of either description for a term which may extend to three years, or with fine, or with both.

Section 125 — Maintenance CrPC

Plain English: Legal provision to prevent vagrancy. If a person neglects to maintain dependent family (wife, children, parents), a Magistrate can order monthly maintenance.

If any person having sufficient means neglects or refuses to maintain... his wife... minor child... or parents... a Magistrate... may order such person to make a monthly allowance...

Section 304B — Dowry Death IPC

Plain English: When a woman dies unnaturally within 7 years of marriage, and it is shown she was harassed for dowry soon before death. The husband/relatives are deemed to have caused the death.

Where the death of a woman is caused by burns, bodily injury, or occurs otherwise than under normal circumstances within seven years of marriage, and it is shown that soon before her death she was subjected to cruelty or harassment for dowry, such death shall be called ‘dowry death’...
