Criminal Law

Section 307 IPC

Attempt to Murder

What does Section 307 say?

"Whoever does any act with such intention or knowledge, and under such circumstances that, if he by that act caused death, he would be guilty of murder..."

Section 307 punishes the attempt to commit murder. Even if the victim survives, the act of trying to kill them is a serious offence. It applies when:

  • There is an act done with the intention to cause death.
  • The act is done with knowledge that it is likely to cause death.
  • The act goes beyond mere preparation and is a direct step towards execution.

Punishment Prescribed

  • Imprisonment for Life, or
  • Imprisonment up to 10 years, and
  • In either case, the offender may also be liable to a fine.

Note: If hurt is caused to the victim during the attempt, the punishment can be more severe (up to life imprisonment).

Key Facts at a Glance

Feature Details
Nature of Offence Cognizable, Non-bailable (Serious offence)
Trial Court Court of Session
Relevant Sections Sec 307 (Attempt), Sec 300 (Murder definition), Sec 302 (Murder punishment)

Difference: Section 307 vs Section 302

Section 302 punishes completed murder (where death has actually occurred). Section 307 punishes the attempt to commit murder (where the victim survives). The key difference is the result of the act, but the intention to kill must be present in both.

Simple Examples

Example A (Attempt to Murder):

A fires a gun at B intending to kill him. The bullet misses or only grazes B's arm. B survives. Since the intent was to kill and the act was done, Section 307 applies.

Example B (Grievous Hurt vs Attempt):

A hits B on the legs with a stick to break his bones but does not intend to kill him. This is likely Grievous Hurt (Sec 325/326), not Attempt to Murder (Sec 307), because the intention to cause death is missing.

Landmark Supreme Court Cases

  • State of Maharashtra v. Mayer Hans George
    A key case discussing the distinction between "preparation" (planning) and "attempt" (execution).
  • Gian Singh v. State of Punjab
    Considered the importance of mens rea (guilty intention) regarding the nature of injuries in attempt cases.
  • Virsa Singh v. State of Punjab
    While primarily a murder case, it is crucial for understanding how courts determine "intention" based on the weapon used and injury caused.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the punishment for attempt to murder?

Imprisonment for life, or imprisonment up to 10 years, plus a fine.

Is attempt to murder bailable?

Generally, no. It is a non-bailable offence. Bail depends entirely on the court's discretion and the facts of the case.

How is it different from causing grievous hurt?

The main difference is intention. Section 307 requires an "intention to cause death." Grievous hurt (Sec 320/326) involves serious injury but without the specific intent to kill.
