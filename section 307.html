What does Section 307 IPC say?

Section 307 of the Indian Penal Code punishes anyone who attempts to commit murder. Attempting to kill someone — even if the victim does not die — is a serious offence under this section.

Plain-English definition

An act will fall under Section 307 when:

  • There is an act done with the intention to cause the death of a person, or
  • The act is done with knowledge that it is likely to cause death, and
  • The act goes beyond mere preparation and moves towards execution (i.e., a direct step was taken to cause death).

Punishment under Section 307

  • Imprisonment for life, or
  • Imprisonment up to 10 years, and
  • In either case, the offender may also be liable to a fine.

Note: If the attempt causes hurt to the victim, courts may award a lighter term depending on the facts. The exact punishment depends on the gravity of the attempt and the circumstances.

Key facts at a glance

FeatureDetails

Trial courtSessions Court

Difference: Section 307 vs Section 302

Section 302 punishes completed murder (death has occurred). Section 307 punishes an attempt to commit murder where the victim survives. The key difference is whether death actually occurred and the stage of the offender's action.

Simple examples

Example A (Attempt to Murder): A fires a gun at B intending to kill; B survives by chance. → Section 307 applies.

Example B (Murder): A stabs B with intent and B dies. → Section 302 applies.

Landmark cases

  • State of Maharashtra v. Mayer Hans George — discussed the distinction between preparation and execution in attempt offences.
  • Gian Singh v. State of Punjab — considered mens rea (intention) for attempt cases.
  • Virsa Singh v. State of Punjab — relevant for understanding intention and severity in violent offences.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is the punishment for attempt to murder (Section 307)?
Imprisonment for life, or imprisonment up to 10 years, and the offender may also be fined.
Is attempt to murder bailable?
Generally non-bailable, given the serious nature of the offence; bail depends on facts and court discretion.
How is attempt to murder different from causing grievous hurt?
The key is intention to kill. Causing grievous hurt may not involve intent to kill and will attract different sections (e.g., Section 325/326).
